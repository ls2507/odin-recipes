Recipe for Pasta

  1. Gather the ingredients:
    • 8 ounces (225 grams) of dried pasta (e.g., spaghetti, penne, fettuccine)
    • Salt (for boiling water)
    • Water (for boiling pasta)
    • Sauce of your choice (e.g., marinara, Alfredo, pesto)
    • Optional toppings or additions (e.g., cooked meat, vegetables, grated cheese, herbs)
  2. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water.
  3. Add the dried pasta to the boiling water and cook it according to the package instructions until it reaches the desired level of doneness (usually around 8-12 minutes). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. While the pasta is cooking, prepare the sauce of your choice. If using a store-bought sauce, heat it in a separate saucepan over medium heat. If making a homemade sauce, follow your preferred recipe.
  5. Once the pasta is cooked, carefully drain it in a colander or use a pasta scoop to remove it from the boiling water.
  6. Transfer the cooked pasta to a large serving bowl or individual plates.
  7. Pour the prepared sauce over the pasta and toss to coat the noodles evenly. Add any additional toppings or additions at this stage.
  8. Serve the pasta hot and, if desired, garnish with grated cheese or fresh herbs.

ENJOY!
